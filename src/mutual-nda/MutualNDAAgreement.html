2) OVERVIEW

The parties intend to disclose information to each other for the purpose of conducting business, and they wish to protect that information. Therefore, the parties agree as follows.

ARTICLE 1 – CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

  1. For the purpose of this agreement, confidential information shall mean any information that is commercially sensitive and/or proprietary in nature, including, but not limited to:
    1. Existing or proposed technologies, products, and services.
    2. Existing or proposed business arrangements and contacts.
    3. Financial information.

ARTICLE 2 – DISCLOSURE BETWEEN PARTIES

  1. Confidential information may be disclosed either verbally or in writing between the parties.
    1. For convenience, the parties shall endeavour to identify and highlight confidential information, at the time of disclosure.

ARTICLE 3 – THE USE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

  1. The parties agree that unless specifically authorised in writing by the other, they will:
    1. Use confidential information solely for the purpose of evaluating proposed arrangements between the parties.
    2. Promptly, upon request of the other party, return or destroy any tangible material concerning such confidential information.

ARTICLE 4 – NON DISCLOSURE

  1. Each party agrees that it will:
    1. Not disclose confidential information to the public or to each other’s competitors.
    2. Safeguard the confidential information.
    3. Only provide employees with access to confidential information on a need-to-know basis.
    4. Not disclose or disseminate the confidential information for their own benefit or the benefit of others, except as authorised in writing by the other party.

ARTICLE 4 – NO LICENCE

  1. Nothing contained in this agreement shall be construed as granting or conferring any rights to licence.

ARTICLE 5 – NO OBLIGATION

  1. The furnishing of confidential information in accordance with this agreement shall not obligate either party to enter into any further agreement or negotiation with the other party.
